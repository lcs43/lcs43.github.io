To calculate the depth of water applied you need to apply the equation

qt=ad  

where:

q= cfs  = gpm/448 = the average flow rate measure by the flume during the irrigation time period
t= time  in hr that the irrigation water is applied to the field
a= the area of the field in acres
d = depth of application in inches.

The field are normally rectangles so the area is the width* length in ft/ 43560. Example a field is 500 ft wide and 100 furrows of 3.5 (42 inches) ft width are irrigated. The area is 500ft*350ft/ 43560=4.01 ac. The flow is 2500 gpm= 2500/448=5.58cfs. The time of the irrigation is 7 hr.

d=qt/a= 5.58*4.01/7=3.2 inches depth of application
